What makes this different

A Nice-Looking Website
and a Website That

Most small business websites are basically digital brochures. They tell you the business exists, maybe list the services, and that's it. There's no real pull to them. The question everyone is asking

Why Not Just Use AI
to Build the Website?

Honestly, it's a fair question. AI can put together something that looks like a website pretty fast. But here's where it falls apart: it doesn't know your business. It doesn't know why a particular type of client hesitates before calling someone like you, or what they're actually looking for when they land on your page. It pulls patterns from what already exists on the internet and reproduces them which means you end up with something that looks like every other website in your category. That's exactly the problem.

What AI gives you

A website that's structurally fine and completely generic. The copy could belong to any business. The layout could belong to any business. Nothing about it says anything specific about yours and clients notice that, even if they can't explain why.

What I give you

Something built around your actual business and the specific people you're trying to reach. Every decision has a reason behind it. And because I'm thinking about it rather than generating it, the result ends up feeling like it belongs to you.

From first message to live website

What Actually Happens
After You Message Me.

There are four stages. Nothing jumps ahead, I've found that skipping the thinking upfront always causes problems later. So the process is linear, and each stage is done properly before the next one starts.

01

I Learn About Your Business

We talk. I ask a lot of questions: about your clients, your competitors, what you feel your current website is failing at. Most people find this part surprisingly useful on its own. By the end of it, I have a clear picture of what the website actually needs to do.

02

Planning the Structure First

Before any design work begins, I map out how the site should flow. Which sections go where, what each one is responsible for, how the whole thing moves a visitor toward getting in touch with you. Structure first. Visuals after.

03

Design and Copy at the Same Time

I don't design first and write later. They're built together because they affect each other. The way something is laid out changes how the words land, and the words change how the design needs to be arranged. Doing them separately usually shows.

04

You Review It, We Fix What Needs Fixing, Then It's Yours

You go through the site, tell me what's not right, and we sort it out. Once you're happy with it, I hand it over fully tested across devices and ready to go live. No loose ends.

Questions I Get Asked.
Answered Straight.

How long does it take to build my website?

Usually 3 to 5 days once we actually start. It depends on how complex the project is and how quickly we can go back and forth. I'll give you a realistic timeline before anything begins so you're not guessing. I won't rush it to hit an arbitrary deadline that's how you end up with something average.

Do you only work with businesses in India?

For now, yes. My focus is on service businesses in India, that's the context I understand well and where I think I can do the most useful work. Everything happens over WhatsApp, so where you're located within India doesn't matter.

Why is there no pricing on the website?

Because a five-page website for a local tutor and a full redesign for a multi-service clinic are completely different projects. A flat package price either overcharges one or underpays for the other. I scope each project based on what it actually needs, and we talk about pricing early in the conversation so there are no surprises.

Can you redesign my existing website instead of building from scratch?

Yes. I go through your current site first and figure out what's working, what isn't, and what's probably costing you enquiries right now. Some sites need a full rebuild. Some just need specific things fixed. I won't recommend more work than the situation actually calls for.

I've never hired a web designer before. Where do I even start?

Just send me a message on WhatsApp. You don't need to prepare a brief or have everything figured out. We'll have a short conversation about your business and what you're trying to solve, and I'll tell you honestly whether I think I can help and what that would look like if I can.

Why not just use Wix or Squarespace?

Those tools are fine if you just need something online. But they're designed for speed and simplicity not for thinking carefully about why your specific type of client does or doesn't reach out. You end up making decisions based on what looks good in the template, not based on what actually works for your audience. If that distinction doesn't matter to you, a builder is probably fine. If it does, it's worth doing properly.
